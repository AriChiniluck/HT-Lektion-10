# Sonnet to the Flowers of Spring

Soft dawn unthreads the frost from meadow green,
And wakes shy crocus from its crystal sleep;
Gold daffodils, in fluttering ranks, are seen
Like little torches by the hedgerows keep.
The tulip cups her wine of scarlet flame,
While bluebells spill a veiled and trembling chime;
Sweet hawthorn scatters stars without a name,
A milky way along the lane of time.

The air is stitched with song from unseen throats,
Where nests are woven high in budding eaves;
The breeze bears drifting perfume’s secret notes
Through orchards laced with newly silvered leaves.

So brief their bloom, yet in their tender hours
They teach the heart to rise like springtime flowers.
