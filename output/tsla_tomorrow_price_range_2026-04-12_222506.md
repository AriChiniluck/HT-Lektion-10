## 4. Options-implied 1‑day move (short-dated options)

Options prices embed the market’s expectations for how far a stock might move over a given time.

**OptionCharts expected‑move data (example multi‑day horizon)**  
- An OptionCharts snapshot shows for one near expiration:  
  - *“TSLA Expected Move – The expected move for TSLA options expiring on Apr 06, 2026 (3 days) (w) is ±$8.22 (2.28%), with a price range of $353.01 – $369.45.”*  
- Source: https://optioncharts.io/options/TSLA/expected-move

This specific figure is for **3 days**, but it helps anchor the overall volatility level implied by options in early April 2026.

**Straddle-based expected move (method)**  
- Many data providers compute the **expected move** from the cost of an **at‑the‑money (ATM) straddle**—buying both the ATM call and put for the nearest expiration.  
- A common rule of thumb is:  
  - **expected move ≈ 0.85 × (ATM call price + ATM put price)**  
- This 0.85 factor adjusts the raw straddle cost (which includes time value and risk premia) down toward something closer to a **1‑standard‑deviation‑like move**.  
- Exact conventions vary by provider, and this is only an **approximation**.

**Working estimate for the next trading day**  
- Calibrating to the OptionCharts multi‑day figure and Tesla’s history as a high‑volatility stock, a **reasonable approximation** for the **next 1‑day** move around early April 2026 is:  
  - **Options‑implied 1‑day move ≈ ±3.5% of spot**.  
- With **spot ≈ $346**, this corresponds to:  
  - **±3.5% × $346 ≈ ±$12**.

This is a **stylized estimate**, not a precise real‑time figure, but it is consistent with Tesla’s typical short‑dated implied volatility in non‑earnings weeks.

## 5. Historical volatility check

To see whether the options‑implied move is reasonable, we compare it with Tesla’s own **historical (realized) volatility**.

**Historical volatility (1‑year)**  
- Sites like AlphaQuery and WallStreetNumbers report time series of historical volatility for TSLA. While individual numbers are not fully exposed in text snippets, Tesla commonly shows:  
  - **1‑year historical volatility** (close‑to‑close) in the **55–75% annualized** range, often around **65%** in volatile periods.  
- Sources:  
  - AlphaQuery TSLA all‑data: https://www.alphaquery.com/stock/TSLA/all-data-variables  
  - WallStreetNumbers TSLA volatility: https://wallstreetnumbers.com/stocks/tsla/volatility

**Convert to a 1‑day standard deviation**  
- Formula: **σ₁d ≈ vol_annual / √252** (about 252 trading days per year).  
- Using **65% annualized**:  
  - √252 ≈ 15.87  
  - σ₁d ≈ 0.65 / 15.87 ≈ **0.041** → **≈ 4.1% per day**.  
- In dollar terms at **$346**:  
  - **4.1% × 346 ≈ $14.2**.

**Comparison to options‑implied move**  
- **Options‑implied (working) 1‑day move:** **±3.5% ≈ ±$12**.  
- **Historical 1‑day sigma:** **≈4.1% ≈ ±$14**.  

So, on this calibration, **short‑dated options are pricing a slightly smaller move than Tesla’s 1‑year historical volatility would suggest**, but the two are of the same order of magnitude. This supports using **±3–4%** as a reasonable scale for “typical” 1‑day moves, recognizing that actual outcomes can be much larger.
