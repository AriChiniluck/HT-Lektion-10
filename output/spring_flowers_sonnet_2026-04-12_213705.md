# Spring Flowers in Their First Bright Choir

Soft thawing winds unlace the frosted air,
And crocus spears break through the sodden brown;
Young tulips tilt like candles lit for prayer,
While pear trees breathe in blossom gowns of down.

The rain threads silver through each waiting bud,
And daffodils ring yellow in the light;
Green rises quietly from the waking mud,
As bees stitch clover to the cloth of white.

Where winter’s iron silence held the field,
Now petals murmur, trembling into song;
The lilac’s purple vows at last revealed,
Say softly that the dark was never long.

So, standing in this drift of bloom and breeze,
My heart, like spring, remembers how to ease.
