# Sonnet to the Flowers of Spring

When winter’s weary mantle slips away,  
The earth in gentle sighs awakes to bloom;  
Soft vernal winds through budding branches play,  
And chase the sullen shadows’ lingering gloom.  

Crocus and primrose pierce the thawing ground,  
Each tender spear a vow of brighter hours;  
The violet spills her shy, retiring sound,  
A faint sweet music shaped in purple flowers.  

Fair daffodils, with morning’s golden hair,  
Like candles set to praise the rising sun,  
Do nod and whisper in the fragrant air,  
As if to bless the year so newly begun.  

Thus blooms teach hearts, long numb with frost and fear,  
To hope again when love and light draw near.
